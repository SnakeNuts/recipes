# Easy Vegan Japanese Curry

Easy Japanese curry that happens to be vegan!

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp flour
- 3 tbsp oil
- 1/2 onion, roughly chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 piece ginger, minced (about the same amount as the garlic)
- 2 tbsp curry powder (preferably Japanese but regular yellow works too)
- 500ml broth (vegetable or chicken)
- 1 carrot, roughly chopped
- 3 potatoes, roughly chopped
- 150g mushrooms, quartered
- 250g firm tofu, cubed
- 1/2 apple, grated
- 2 tbsp ketchup
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp cocoa powder

## Method

1. Heat the oil in a Dutch oven or large pot over medium heat. Add the flour and stir continuously for 5-7 minutes until the mixture becomes a caramel color.

2. Add the chopped onion and cook for 7-8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onion softens and begins to caramelize.

3. Stir in the minced garlic, ginger, and curry powder. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until fragrant.

4. Pour in the broth and add the carrot, potatoes, and mushrooms. Stir everything well, ensuring the vegetables are coated with the curry base. Cook for 7-8 minutes, or until the potatoes and carrots are about 80% cooked.

5. Add the cubed tofu, grated apple, ketchup, and soy sauce. Sift the cocoa powder directly into the pot to avoid clumps. Gently stir to combine all the ingredients.

6. Simmer for another 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the vegetables to finish cooking.

7. Serve hot over a bed of steamed rice.
