# Frikandel Speciaal

## Ingredients

### Frikandel
- 300g pork belly
- 400g chicken thighs
- 40g breadcrumbs
- 1 onion
- 3/4 tbsp piment
- 1 1/2 tbsp garlic powder
- 3 tbsp onion powder
- 1 1/2 tbsp white pepper
- 3/4 tsp nutmeg
- 3/4 tbsp salt
- 3/4 tbsp cilantro powder
- 75ml heavy cream

### Curry Sauce
- 500g tomatoes
- 3 red onions
- 50g light caster sugar
- 100ml apple cider vinegar
- 2 garlic cloves
- 35g tomato paste
- Splash Worcestershire sauce
- 1/2 lemon juice
- 1/2 tsp star anise
- 3 tbsp curry powder
- 1 tbsp ginger powder
- 1 tbsp black pepper
- 1 tsp salt
- 1 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp mustard powder
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp piment
- Optional cornstarch slurry

### Mayonnaise
- 1 tsp mustard
- 240ml neutral oil
- 1 egg
- Pinch of salt
- Squeeze of lemon

### Topping
- White onion

## Method

1. To a blender add the pork belly and chicken and blend well until really smooth.

2. Go in with all the spices, heavy cream, bread crumbs and an onion. Blend again.

3. Now form long rolls and roll them tight in some plastic wrap.

4. Fold the ends of the plastic wrap and wrap another piece around it so you are sure it will not get loose.

5. Bring a pot with water to a simmer (about 70°C) and let sit for about 10 minutes.

6. Take them out of the water, let cool down and remove the plastic wrap.

7. For the curry mince the onion, tomato and garlic.

8. Saute the onion and garlic, before adding the tomato, sugar, star anise powder, curry powder, ginger powder, black pepper, cayenne, piment, cinnamon and mustard powder. Stew for a while, about 40 minutes.

9. Press the sauce to a sieve and let boil another time. Bring to taste with lemon juice, salt and optional some worcestershire sauce.

10. Make the mayo by mixing an egg, oil, mustard, salt and a squeeze of lemon juice into a blender bottle and let emulsify from the bottom to the top.

11. Bring a pan with oil to 175°C and fry the frikandellen for about 1-2 minutes.

12. Serve with the mayo, the curry and a finely minced white onion.
