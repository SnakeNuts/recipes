# Creamy Chicken and Chorizo

Serves 3-4.

## Ingredients

### Chicken
- 600g boneless skinless chicken thighs (preferably 6 even-sized thighs)
- 1 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/4 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/8-1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tbsp olive oil

### Sauce
- 1 small white onion
- 100g diced Spanish chorizo
- 2 garlic cloves
- 60ml dry white wine
- 180ml low-salt chicken stock
- 180ml double/heavy cream
- 20g freshly grated Parmesan
- 1/2 bunch fresh basil (approximately 15g)
- 175g baby plum tomatoes

## Method

1. Season both sides of the thighs. Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add thighs and fry both sides until lightly charred/cooked right through the centre (4-5 mins each side depending on size). If they start charring too aggressively just lower the heat slightly. Remove and place to one side. Lower heat to medium.

2. During this time finely dice the onion and garlic, halve the tomatoes and roughly dice the basil. If you need to grate the parmesan, make the stock and dice the chorizo do this now too (make sure it's diced nice and small).

3. Add onion to the leftover fat in the pan and fry until it begins to soften. Add chorizo and fry for 2 mins, then fry garlic for 30 secs. Add wine, raise the heat and bring to a rapid simmer. Cook for around 2 mins until it almost completely evaporates. Lower heat slightly.

4. Add stock, cream and parmesan and give it a really good stir to blend. Simmer fairly rapidly for 3-4 mins until the sauce thickens, stirring frequently. Add tomatoes, basil and chicken resting juices and gently simmer for a couple of minutes until the tomatoes begin to soften.

5. Add chicken for a min or so to warm then serve.
