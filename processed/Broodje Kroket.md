# Broodje Kroket

A simple looking sandwich that packs a punch. Full of flavour with soft white bread, a crispy outside to the kroket, and gooey, meaty centre.

## Ingredients

- 1kg braising beef (brisket, chuck roast, etc)
- 1 large or 2 small carrots
- 2 onions
- 1 leek
- 4 garlic cloves
- A few sprigs thyme
- A few sprigs rosemary
- A big handful parsley
- 2-3 bay leaves
- 80g butter
- 100g flour + more for breading
- 2+ eggs
- ~100g breadcrumbs
- Nutmeg
- Black pepper
- Salt
- Water

## Method

1. Chunk the beef and veg, peel the garlic cloves.

2. In a Dutch oven (or similar) brown the meat, then the veg, then combine, cover with water, add thyme, rosemary, half the parsley and bay leaves. Season with salt and black pepper. Optionally cover with a cartouche and pop in the oven at 145°C for 3-4 hours, until the beef is super tender.

3. Remove the beef from the pot and shred, strain the liquid (stock) from the pot and save.

4. Melt the butter in a pan, add 100g flour, combine and cook out for 2 minutes. Slowly add the stock while stirring to create a thick sauce. Add the remaining parsley (chopped), grate in some nutmeg and season with salt and black pepper.

5. Combine the sauce and shredded beef, the ratio is up to you, go meaty or saucy. Cover and refrigerate until completely cool, this will probably take a few hours.

6. Once ready, form the kroketten then dredge in flour, then beaten egg, and finally breadcrumbs. Fry at 180°C for 4-5 minutes or until brown, crispy and piping hot.

7. Slice open a soft white bread roll, add mustard (Dutch mustard for bonus points) and place in the kroket. Enjoy immediately.
