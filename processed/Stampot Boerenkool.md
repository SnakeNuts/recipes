# Stampot Boerenkool

Mashed Potatoes with Kale.

Recipe for 4 servings.

## Ingredients

- 1kg potatoes
- 500g shredded kale
- 1 beef stock cube
- 50ml milk
- 25g butter
- 1 tbsp mustard
- Freshly ground pepper
- 1 or 2 smoked sausages
- 100g bacon slices
- 100g cured pork belly (Zeeland-style bacon) slices
- Splash of sunflower oil for frying
- 50g Amsterdam pickled onions (baby pickled onions)
- 50g small gherkins
- Few sprigs of fresh parsley

## Method

1. Peel the potatoes, cut them into equal pieces and place in water so they're just covered. Add a stock cube or some salt if you prefer.

2. Once the water is coming to the boil, add the kale and let it cook with the lid slightly ajar on the pan for 20 minutes.

3. Let the smoked sausages warm through in hot, but not boiling water.

4. Lay the bacon on a baking sheet lined with baking paper and cover with another sheet of baking paper and another baking sheet or oven-safe board, so the bacon stays nice and flat while baking.

5. Bake the bacon in the oven at 200°C with top and bottom heat for approximately 30 minutes until crispy.

6. Fry the cured pork belly with a drop of oil on both sides briefly and keep it warm.

7. Check the potatoes are cooked through, drain the water and put the pan back on low heat.

8. Mash finely with a potato masher and add milk, butter, pepper and mustard. Some people like to add nutmeg too. You can keep the mashed potato and kale warm with the lid on the pan until everything is ready.

9. You can shape the mashed potato and kale nicely using a springform cake ring. But it can of course also just be served as is on a plate.

10. Decorate the mashed potato with sausage pieces, cured pork and crispy bacon. And give it a nice flavour accent with Amsterdam pickled onions and gherkins.

11. A few sprigs of fresh parsley finish it off perfectly.

12. Serve with a jug of gravy or perhaps some extra mustard.
