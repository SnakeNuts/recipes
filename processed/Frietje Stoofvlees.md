# Frietje Stoofvlees

Fries with Braised Beef.

## Ingredients

### Braised Beef (4 servings)
- 1kg braising beef such as chuck roast or beef rib
- 2 onions
- 4 garlic cloves
- 50g butter
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 70g tomato paste
- 1 bottle dark beer (30cl)
- 0.5 litre beef stock
- 10g sprigs of thyme
- 10g sprigs of rosemary
- 3 bay leaves
- 100g ontbijtkoek (Dutch spiced gingerbread cake)
- 2 tbsp mustard

### Fries
- 1kg waxy potatoes
- 2L frying oil

## Method

### Braised Beef
1. Cut the meat into reasonably large cubes and brown in 2 batches in a large braising pan with the butter and olive oil.

2. Do this briefly in a very hot pan; the meat should get a nice colour but it's important that the meat stays red inside and doesn't become tough.

3. Remove from the pan and then add the finely chopped onions and garlic.

4. Fry for 3 minutes and add the tomato paste.

5. Cook for 1 minute and deglaze with the beer.

6. Warm this briefly and then add the meat back together with the stock and herbs and bring to the boil.

7. Once it's boiling, put the pan on a very low heat so it simmers gently rather than boiling vigorously.

8. Now also add the ontbijtkoek with mustard and simmer until the meat falls apart.

9. Do this with the lid partly on the pan (about three-quarters covered).

10. This can happen within 2 hours in some cases but usually takes around 3-4 hours. If it sits all day it becomes even more tender, just make sure it doesn't stick and add a little stock occasionally if needed.

### Fries
1. Wash the potatoes well. Cut them into slices and then into chips/fries.

2. Deep fry at 150°C for 6 minutes, remove from the pan, heat the pan to 180°C and fry until they're golden brown and crispy.

Note: Every stove, pan and extractor hood is different. Because of this, the cooking time can vary slightly for everyone. If the sauce remains very thin, remove some liquid and continue cooking it down. Later you can add the liquid back until you reach the desired thickness.
